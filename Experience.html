Surveying

Road survey

In the preliminary design phase, we conduct comprehensive surveys to gather essential data for the initial road designs, considering factors such as alignment, elevation, and drainage. Alignment involves determining the optimal path for the road, taking into account terrain, existing infrastructure, and environmental impact. Elevation assessment ensures a smooth gradient and safe driving conditions, while effective drainage planning prevents water accumulation and ensures the road's longevity. Moving to the detailed design phase, we create thorough engineering designs, including cross-sections, profiles, and construction plans for the Balam Area in Pokhara. Cross-sections provide detailed views of the road's different material layers and their thickness, while profiles illustrate the road's elevation changes over its length. The construction plans encompass comprehensive specifications and instructions necessary for building the road, ensuring a clear and structured approach to the construction process.

Detail Topographic Survey

A detailed topographic survey has been meticulously conducted for the Research & Innovation Center (RIC) at Pashchimanchal Campus. This comprehensive survey involved a thorough examination of the area's natural and man-made features, including elevations, contours, and existing infrastructure. The primary goal was to capture accurate and precise data that reflects the current terrain and landscape. Using advanced surveying equipment and techniques, every aspect of the site was measured and recorded, from the smallest undulations in the land to significant structural elements. This detailed topographic survey is crucial for various phases of planning and development, providing a solid foundation for future design and construction projects. The collected data will aid in ensuring that any new developments are well-integrated into the existing environment, addressing potential challenges related to elevation changes and drainage. This meticulous approach helps in creating sustainable and efficient designs that align with the campus's vision for growth and innovation.

Cadastral & Hydropower Survey

A comprehensive detailed survey has been conducted for the preparation of a cadastral map sheet, which is essential for accurately documenting property boundaries, land ownership, and other legal land descriptions. This detailed work involved precise measurements and mapping to ensure that all cadastral information is accurately represented, aiding in legal matters, land disputes, and development planning. Additionally, a detailed feasibility topographic survey has been carried out for the construction of a hydropower plant in the Armala area of Pokhara. This survey is vital for assessing the site's suitability for such a large-scale infrastructure project. It involves evaluating the terrain, identifying potential environmental impacts, and determining the best location for various components of the hydropower plant. The topographic data collected helps in understanding the land's contours and elevation changes, which are crucial for the design and construction phases of the hydropower project. By integrating this detailed topographic information, planners and engineers can create a more efficient and sustainable design that maximizes energy production while minimizing environmental disruption.
